Ethical Concerns

Formulating questions

The biggest ethical concern at the stage of formulating questions is welfare and happiness. Type 2 diabetes is a painful disease. Patients and their families are bothered by it. With the research, they may have to face the problem again, making them more stressed or affecting their mental health. In addition, since it has strong relations with obesity and what people ingest, the profits of companies and manufacturers may be influenced, which means the research may affect the welfare of organizations.

Data collection and process

At this stage, since this is an on-campus project, we do not have IRB approval or IRB certification, which means we do not get informed consent. Therefore, we could not further develop our research. Moreover, the data we have in the American Community Survey involves age, gender, income, and ethnicity, which could be biased and harmful to discuss. Besides, we have a lot of categorical data. The edges among categories may be blurred and not fair, which may also affect our models.

Analysis and Results

The data we have are not biased. To reduce bias, we did not talk about age and gender in our analyses, but we analyzed the relationship between income and diabetes prevalence, and the relationship between the obesity rate and race, which could be biased and harmful. Also, we cannot make sure we did not conduct any biased hypotheses and analyses on the results of our models. Therefore, there may be some bias issues as well as fairness issues.
